# To where is this road taking me?

## My Speech

To where is this road taking me?
わたしの　すすむべき　みち

When I was 10 years old, my mom used to ask me and my brothers what we want to be when we grow up. My eldest brother said he wants to be doctor. My second eldest said he wants to be an engineer. I chose to be a lawyer. Of course we have no idea what we were talking about.

１０さいの　とき、ははが　わたしと　きょうだいに　しつもんを　しました。
「おとなに　なったら、なにに　なりたいの？」
いちばん　うえの　あには、「いしゃに　なりたい！」と　いいました。
にばん　うえの　あには、「ぼくは　エンジニアに　なりたい！」と　いいました。
そして　わたしは、「じゃ、ぼくは　べんごしだ！」といいました。
もちろん、なにを　いったか、みんなは　さっぱり　わかりませんでした。

High school came, and I learned to play a guitar. Here I thought I dreamt of being a rock star. Then I graduated high school. This time I will have to decide what I really want to be. But unfortunately I couldn’t make up my mind. I never knew this time would come and I have to make hard decisions. I ended up depending on my parent’s decisions. So as advised, I took two college entrance exams – one that would lead me to a nursing career, and the other to the field of computers. I passed both exams, but as usual I made a random decision – I took the Computer Science course. Well, there may be a few reasons why I took this course. I thought computers are cool, and nowadays everyone is into it. But whatever course I would take, it didn’t matter to me at that time until college.

こうこうせいの　とき、ギターが　ひけるように　なりました。
そのとき、ロックスターに　なりたく　なったの　です。
こうこうせい　を　そつぎょうした　あと、まじめな　けつだんを　しなければ　なりませんでした。
なかなか　きめられなかったので、ははに　アドバイスを　もとめました。
「２つの　だいがくにゅうしを　うけたら？」と　ははが　いいました。
「そうか！それも　いいですね。」
しかし、かんごがくぶの　にゅうし　にも、コンピューターサイエンスのにゅうし　にも　ごうかくしてしまいました。
いやぁ～　あたまが　よくて　こまりますね～
あのとき、コンピューターの　ことが　ゆうめいで、かっこいいから、コンピューターサイエンスを　べんきょうする　ことに　しました。

We were about 50 freshman students taking the Computer Science course at the University of the Philippines. Years went by and the subjects were getting harder. By the third year, a lot of my classmates have already shifted to other courses. On the fourth year, about 20 regular students were left taking this course. One day it occurred to me that I should wonder if this is the path I really wanted to pursue. I didn’t know how to answer that. All I knew is that I’m already here and I have to face this challenge – be able to graduate in this course, and make my mom and dad proud. And yes, I did accomplish that goal, and I was so happy.

フィリピン　だいがくで、５０人の　クラスメートと　コンピューターサイエンスを　まなびはじめました。
ですが、まいとし、じゅぎょうが　だんだん　むずかしく　なって　いく。。。
３ねんめに、おおくの　どうりょうが　ほかの　がくぶに　てんこうして　しまいました。
４ねんせいに　なって、ついに　２０にんしか　コンピューターサイエンスに　のこって　いなかった。
あの　とき、ふと　おもいました。
「わたしの　すすむべき　みちは、ほんとうに　これで　いいの　かな？」
ぎもんを　かんじはじめたが、おとうさんと　おかあさんは、わたしを　しんじていた。
りょうしんの　ためにも、どうしても　そつぎょうしたい。
そして　べんきょうを　がんばって、ぶじ　そつぎょう　できた。
もくひょうが　たっせいできて、すごく　うれしかったの　です。
But my life didn’t stop there; the real world is waiting for me. I took my first job as a programmer in a small company. And I realized that all the things I learned in school are not enough, there is still much more to learn – more technologies, more programming languages and concepts, more books to read. Learning never stops and this job is driving me crazy. But years later, I get used to my job and eventually I get to like and enjoy the fruits of it.

ですが、じんせいは　だいがくどまり　では　ありません。
ちいさな　かいしゃの　プログラマーとして、はじめて　しゅうしょくしました。
でも、べきょうする　ことが　まだまだ　たくさんある！
あたらしい　ぎじゅつ、
あたらしい　げんご、
あたらしい　かんがえかたの　まいにち。
たいへん　でしたが、プログラマーの　しごとが　すきに　なって、
いま　ＮＳＰに　はいることが　できて　うれしいです。
How do you decide whether you took the right path or not? My parents advised me to take a course that would lead me to a high-paying job. In my academic life, I have learned to strive hard and be successful. I thought about these things and pondered whether I made the right choice.
Then I thought about college. I thought about my good friends who shifted to other courses since they didn’t do well in the computer science course. Some of my friends stopped schooling, and some even entered parenthood. Then I thought about the people I meet every day. The jeepney drivers who drive their wheels early in the morning to send the students to schools and the workers to their companies. The people who tended their stores, waiting for customers to come and buy their goods. The street children who roam around the busy streets hoping to meet kind-hearted people. I guess they didn’t choose the path they are already in, they were only compelled to do so. But somehow, they still get along and smile with the people around them.

わたしたちは　ほんとうに　すすむべき　みちを　あゆんで　いますか？
ちがう　がくぶに　てんこうした　だいがくの　クラスメート。
だいがくを　やめて　かぞくを　はじめた　ゆうじん。
あさはやく　しごとをする　ジープニーの　うんてんしゅさん。
ざっかやで　おきゃくさんを　まつ　おばちゃん。
しんせつな　ひとを　まつ　こじき。
みんなは、いまの　みちを　じぶんで　えらべなかったと　おもいます。
それでも、ほほえみながら　せいかつを　おくっています。

They say “we are what we are because we chose to become one”. But even though each of us hits our own road of life, at some point our roads merge with other people’s roads. Life then becomes an adventure and you can never tell what happens next. Life isn’t a story book on which you can flip to the last page. But as long as you’re alive, there will be so many things in life that you will need to decide. You’ll just have to choose what makes you happy in the end.
I never dreamed of being a programmer when I was young, I only dreamt of being a rock star. But despite the twist of fate, I’m still happy because before I got where I am right now, I’m sure I have made the right choices. I hope you have made the right choices, too.

じぶんの　いきるみちが　えらべる　わたしたちは、ひじょうに　めぐまれています。
じんせいは　わかれみちが　おおい　ですが、しあわせに　なる　みちを　えらべば　いいです。
「べんごしに　なろうか」「ロックスターに　なろうか」と、
むかし　まよいましたが、
いまの　しあわせな　じぶんを　みて、
きっと　ただしい　みちを　えらんだ　のだ　と　おもいます。
あしたは　なにが　おこるか　わかりませんが、
みなさんと　いっしょに、じんせい　という　ぼうけんを　たのしみたい　と　おもいます。

ごせいちょう、ありがとうございました。
